The National Education Policy (NEP) 2020 is a comprehensive framework for the development of education in India. It aims to transform the Indian education system by introducing innovative reforms to improve quality, accessibility, and inclusiveness. The NEP envisions a holistic approach to education, emphasizing critical thinking, vocational training, and the integration of technology. It seeks to provide a robust foundation for lifelong learning and skill development, ensuring that every student is prepared to meet the challenges of a rapidly changing world. This policy is set to revolutionize the educational landscape, addressing various challenges and paving the way for a more equitable and progressive system.

When loneliness in a faraway land hit him, he came back home. His Delhi-based parents, Rashmi and Rakesh Arya, had also moved to their hometown in Shamsabad, Agra (Uttar Pradesh) during the lockdown. “After coming home, I decided not to go back again. I wanted to pursue something here, and organic farming was one of the options since our family owns around 3 acres of land here in Shamsabad,” says 27-year-old Rishabh. Around the same time, his younger brother Ayush Gupta completed his BBA from the University of London and returned to India.
